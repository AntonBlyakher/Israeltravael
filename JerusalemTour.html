Information about the Area

Jerusalem is a truly unique city where three major religions converge. This offers tourists a wonderful opportunity to explore the rich diversity of cultures, architecture, and spiritual history.

Old City

We'll explore the Jewish, Christian, and Muslim quarters, each with its own distinct character and historical significance. As we walk through these ancient streets, you'll experience the heritage of all three faiths.

Mount Zion

Located just south of the Old City, Mount Zion once lay within Jerusalem's walls during the time of the Savior. It is mentioned in the Old Testament as a place chosen by God. Here, King David established his palace after conquering the city of Jebus, and moved the Ark of the Covenant. The site is home to the religious synagogue dedicated to David and the Nabi-Daoud Mosque.

Via Dolorosa (The Path of Sorrow)

We will walk along this historic route, retracing the final steps of Jesus Christ before his crucifixion. The path weaves through Christian and Muslim neighborhoods, offering a fascinating blend of cultures along the way.

Church of the Holy Sepulchre

One of the holiest sites in Christianity, this church is revered as the place of Jesus' crucifixion, burial, and resurrection. It is divided among six Christian denominations, each maintaining its traditions.

Western Wall

The holiest site for Jews, this wall is a remnant of the First and Second Temples and symbolizes hope for the construction of the Third Temple. It also stands as a poignant reminder of the victims of persecution throughout history.
